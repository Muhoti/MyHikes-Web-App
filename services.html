Picnics and Adventures

Ever imagined cycling up a long stretch of a cool road or say up the hills of Ngong? What other better way is there than spending a whole Saturday biking up the Ngong hills?
Get the directions now on the map by searching 'Ngong hills' on the search tool and fix the navigation icons to your location and destination and find yourself doing some pretty exercise up the Ngong hills!
Consider taking a nature stroll through the Karura forest or having a walk down Oloolua Nature trail. You can even consider visiting the caves of Paradise lost and enjoy the darkness and sound of bats as they flip deep in the holes! You certainly can't get a better feeling than descending those walls of the steep caves to the very bottom of holes kilometres long.
Or is it some great height you have been dreaming of for so long? Did you know you could watch all of Nairobi from the crest of KICC. Run there to hitch up your ride to the very top of one of Nairobi's tallest buildings and enjoy a bird's view right at its helipad! But take care not to have helicopters landing on your head!

Statues and Monuments

Have a free educational day of Kenya's history by taking a walk into the oldest buildings popularly known for the colonialists administration as they colonised Kenya.
Walk into Kipande house and see where African's were required by the Mzungu to register and collect identification documents porpularly known as 'Kipande'.
Or are you a lover of archived documents? Walk in right into the Kenya National Archives at Moi avenue, right opposite Kencom and get to touch and have a feel of ancient government documents dating back to the 1950s when Kenya was under the white man's rulership and have a look at how the gpovernment then was like.
Or are you a lover of artefacts? Just explore through the National Archives hall and have a view of Murumbi Gallery which is currently the largest Pan-Africa art Gallery in Africa.
Don't forget to walk by Tom Mboya Street and Kimathi street to have a breathe-taking view as you come to one on one meeting with the historic hero's who won Kenya by their blood and established it on its fortresses even by commitments and convictions they upheld virtous to their deaths.

Wildlife and Animals

Ever come one on one with any of the members of the big 5? Get yourself to the Nairobi National Park and you'll certainly not miss meeting with some of them.
Or is it crocodiles that you have always feared seeing their hundreds of sharp teeth and imagined one of them grasping your leg? You'll sure enjoy watching them at close proximity feeding on bloody chickens and not fearing them attacking you. Just walk to Mamba village and have a day together with the crocodiles.
Mark you, you can also consider water biking, at the beautiful pools around.
